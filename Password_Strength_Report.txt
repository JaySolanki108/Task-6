4. Analysis and Observations
•The evaluation results show that password strength increases significantly with greater length and complexity. Passwords that include a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters are more resistant to attacks. Additionally, long passphrases provide high security while remaining easier to remember.

5. Common Password Attacks
5.1 Brute Force Attack
•A brute force attack involves systematically trying all possible character combinations until the correct password is discovered. Longer and more complex passwords increase the time required to successfully perform this attack.

5.2 Dictionary Attack
•A dictionary attack uses a predefined list of commonly used words and passwords to guess the correct password. Passwords containing common words or patterns are more vulnerable to this attack.


6. Best Practices for Strong Passwords
•Use passwords with a minimum length of 12 characters
•Avoid using personal or easily guessable information
•Include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters
•Prefer passphrases over short passwords
•Enable multi-factor authentication for additional security

7. Conclusion
•This task demonstrates that password length and complexity play a crucial role in protecting user accounts. Strong passwords and passphrases significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access and enhance overall cybersecurity.
